# West-Africa-GDP-Analyses-1960---2016-

# 📋 NGO Summary Report: West African Economic Development (1960-2016)

## 1. Project Motivation
This analysis was conducted to assist the NGO in understanding the historical economic trajectories of seven key West African nations. By comparing annual growth against a regional baseline, we can identify periods of resilience, rapid expansion, and volatility.

## 2. Methodology
- **Regional Baseline:** Calculated as the mean annual growth rate of Ghana, Nigeria, Cote d'Ivoire, Togo, Benin, Guinea, and Burkina Faso for each year.
- **Data Alignment:** All datasets were synchronized to a 1960-2016 timeline. The initial 1960 records were used as a baseline and excluded from percentage growth averages to ensure mathematical accuracy.
- **Stability Metric:** Measured using the Standard Deviation of annual growth rates to identify economies with consistent versus volatile development.

## 3. Country-Specific Insights

### 🇳🇬 Nigeria: The High-Growth Powerhouse
Nigeria exhibits the highest average annual growth in the region (~11.64%). However, it is also the most volatile (Std Dev: 27.76), with extreme peaks such as **2010**. Its performance significantly drives the regional average.

### 🇬🇭 Ghana: Consistent Outperformer
Ghana has maintained a robust growth profile, notably peaking in **2006**. Its stability is higher than Nigeria's, making it a benchmark for steady development in the region.

### 🇨🇮 Cote d'Ivoire: Resilience through Change
Despite historical challenges, Cote d'Ivoire remains a core economic pillar of the region, with its growth trends often mirroring the regional average closely.

### 🇧🇫 Burkina Faso & 🇧🇯 Benin: The Models of Stability
These nations represent the most stable economies in the group (lowest Std Dev). While their peak growth rates are lower than the 'powerhouses,' their consistency provides a predictable environment for development programs.

### 🇬🇳 Guinea & 🇹🇬 Togo: Emerging Trends
Both countries show significant growth potential but have historically experienced more fluctuations than their more stable neighbors, with Guinea showing a notable peak in **2007**.

## 4. High-Level Conclusions
- **The Mid-2000s Boom:** Most countries in this group experienced a synchronized economic 'super-cycle' during the mid-2000s, suggesting high sensitivity to global commodity prices or regional trade improvements.
- **Volatility Management:** The NGO should consider that high-growth figures in the region often come with high risk (volatility). Programs in Nigeria or Guinea may require more robust contingency planning compared to those in Benin or Burkina Faso.
